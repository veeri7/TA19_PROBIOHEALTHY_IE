Symptoms of Human Papillomavirus (HPV)



Overview

Types of HPV infection cause warts, and some can even cause different types of cancer. HPV infection commonly causes skin or mucous membrane growths (warts). Most HPV infections don't lead to cancer, but some types are.

HPV causes cancers

There are six main cancers that caused by HPV infection: cervical cancer, vulvar cancer, vaginal cancer, oral cancer, penile cancer and anal cancer.






Types of Warts

In most cases, when HPV attack people's immune system, it will create warts on people's body. There are four main types of warts appeared on people's body depend on types of HPV:

  • Gential Warts: these appear as flat lesions, small cauliflower-like bumps or tiny stemlike protrusions.
  • Common Warts: these appear as rough, raised bumps and usually occur on the hands and fingers.
  • Plantar Warts: these are hard, grainy growths that usually appear on the heels or balls of your feet.
  • Flat Warts: these are flat-topped, slightly raised lesions.
