How Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Spread



Overview

The HPV is transferred theoretically by skin-to-skin contact. Thus, HPV infection occurs when the virus enters your body.

Most HPV infections are caused by sexual intercourse, anal sex and other skin-to-skin contact. Some HPV infections are contracted through oral sex. Baby also may get infection if a pregnant have HPV.

Path of HPV (Sexual routes)

HPV infects the skin and genital area. In many cases leading to a variety of genital, anal, and oropharyngeal cancers in men and women.

Path of HPV (Nonsexual routes)

Most infected individuals are unaware that they are infected and may unknowingly spread the HPV.

The documentation of nonsexual routes is rare, but it can be categorized：

  • Verticle transmission (e.g. mother to newborn)
  • Fomites (e.g. Unsterilized medical gloves)
