Types of Human Papillomavirus (HPV)



Overview

There are more than 150 HPV types discovered in the world. DON'T WORRY, most types of HPV don't pose a serious to health. But you still need to be concern that at least 10 types of HPV are in high risk that can cause related cancers.


Terms of Terminologies:
  • Mucosal: tend to infect cells of the moist surface layers that line organs and cavities of the body.
  • Cutaneous: infect the skin and can cause warts there.
  • High Risk Types: strains include HPV 16 and 18, which cause about 70% of cervical cancers.
  • Low Risk Types: strains, such as HPV 6 and 11, cause about 90% of genital warts.
  • Common Warts: cause little symptoms, and the body clears the infection on its own in a few years.

List of HPV and related Diseases:

Based on the association with cervical cancer and precursor lesions, HPVs can also be grouped to high-risk and low-risk HPV types. Low-risk HPV types include types 6, 11, 42, 43, and 44. High-risk HPV types include types 16, 18, 31, 33, 34, 35, 39, 45, 51, 52, 56, 58, 59, 66, 68, and 70. Included in the high-risk group are some HPV types that are less frequently found in cancers
