Why HPV Vaccine is Important



Overview

Under the National Immunisation Program (NIP) program, Australia government strongly encourage residents to take vaccine and most of vaccines are free of PR/Citizens.

Not only for HPV, but also for other viruses, vaccines are one of the most convenient and safest preventive care measures available, like eating healthy foods, exercising, and getting regular check-ups.

Statistics of HPV vaccine

According to a research issued by Cancer Cancer, almost two thirds in 5,000 random sample group have been affected with HPV if they refused to take vaccine.

Reseachers also approved that people who take vaccine can reduce the risk of HPV infection. Although vaccine cannot be the 100% guaranteed method, data approves that the vaccine can protect vaccine effectively.

Reactions after Vaccination

Vaccines, like any medicine, can have side effects. Common side effects from the HPV shot are mild and go away quickly. The most common side effects include: headache, dizziness, fever, etc.
