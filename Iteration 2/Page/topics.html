Topics

Immunisation is a simple, safe and effective way of protecting people against harmful diseases before they come into contact with them. Immunisation not only protects individuals, but also others in the community, by reducing the spread of preventable diseases.

Cardiovascular

Heart disease can make it harder for you to fight off certain diseases or make it more likely that you will have serious complications from certain diseases.

Breast Cancer

Several studies are testing treatment vaccines for breast cancer, given alone or with other therapies. Other researchers are working to get prevention vaccines into clinical trials.

Human-Papilloma Virus

HPV is the most common sexually transmitted infection in Australia. More than 40 HPV types can infect the genital areas of men and women.

Lung cancer

Lung cancer treatment vaccines in clinical trials target antigens. These include MAGE-3, which is found in 42% of lung cancers and NY-ESO-1, found in 30% of lung cancers. Others target antigens such as p53, survivin, and MUC1.

Anal Cancer

Anal cancer is an uncommon type of cancer that occurs in the anal canal. The anal canal is a short tube at the end of your rectum through which stool leaves your body. Anal cancer can cause signs and symptoms such as rectal bleeding and anal pain.

Brain Cancer

In an ongoing phase III clinical trial, a brain cancer vaccine has significantly prolonged the life of participants with glioblastoma.
