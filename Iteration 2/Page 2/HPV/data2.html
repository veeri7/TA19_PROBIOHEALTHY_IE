Caveats/footnotes:

  • High-grade abnormality detection is defined as the number of women who have a high-grade abnormality detected per 1,000 women screened; a high-grade abnormality is defined as a histology result of HS03 (CIN NOS, CIN II and CIN III) or HE03 (endocervical dysplasia and adenocarcinoma in situ).
  • Data are based on all women screened in each jurisdiction.
  • These data exclude women who have opted off the cervical screening register.
  • Periods based on calendar years (1 January to 31 December).
  • Source: AIHW 2018. Cervical screening in Australia 2018. Cat. no. CAN 111. Canberra: AIHW.
